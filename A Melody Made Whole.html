Unspoken: Poems of Love and Memory

A Melody Made Whole

About this poem

This poem is a touching narrative poem that traces the journey of friendship, from deep loneliness and isolation in the classroom to the discovery of two warm, bubbly souls who brought light and belonging. After seasons of blossoming together and eventual painful separation, the speaker holds onto quiet hope through years of distance. The poem beautifully culminates in a joyful reunion that mends the broken circle and rekindles an unbreakable bond. A heartfelt tribute to the resilience of true friendship and the healing power of reconnection.

I was a lone wolf in the classroom,
An isolated soul among the crowd.
With no one to share my joys or sorrows,
I battled solitude in silence.

Then one day, I found two bubbly girls,
Innocent at heart, with smiles like pearls.
They extended the hand of friendship
With warmth, grace, and kindness.

I welcomed them into my lonely life at once,
Like a weary traveler finally finding home,
Becoming part of a story
I felt I had always known.

As the seasons passed,
Our friendship blossomed like a fresh spring flower.
But the day came when we parted with smiles,
And our petals scattered upon the ocean breeze.

I left the garden behind,
Watching it fade into the darkness.
I searched for my friends wherever I could,
Through years of distance and change.

Though bitter frost covered the path,
A quiet hope remained within me,
That one day, spring might return.

And then, at last, the day arrived.
I found one of those cherished souls.
The moment sparkled like a diamond,
Caught in the light of dawn.

We talked as though time had never passed,
Untying years of silent knots.
Then came the third soul,
And the circle was finally made whole.

A broken melody found its missing chord,
And hearts once separated were reunited.
Together again, we rekindled a flame that would not fade,
While years of silence melted into joy.

Today, my heart overflows with gratitude,
For some treasures are not lost forever.
Time may scatter petals across distant shores,
Yet true friendship finds its way home again.

●— This piece is dedicated to my two long-lost friends—●

Arshia Sultana
