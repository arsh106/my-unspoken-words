Unspoken: Poems of Love and Memory

Fragments of an Unwritten Ending

I came across your pictures recently,
and sudden emotional turmoil rained down on me,
A storm of grief, curiosity, guilt, and compassion.

The old, unfinished chapter of my life
stirred awake, unearthing the most cherished memory
I had buried deep in my heart for decades.

At the sight of your image,
my mind keeps asking:
Is that you, the boy I once knew?
The one who carried a magical smile?

Now I see a stranger,
a weathered and aged man.

Yet somewhere inside,
I still see you as a source of light that holds quiet value in my life.
You were a gentle pause in my life’s long story,
a peaceful memory I kept untouched.

But I fear reaching out.
I dread the pain it might awaken in us both.
I must protect the fragile comfort of our silence,
the careful boundaries we’ve drawn.

Perhaps you no longer carry the ghost of our past.
My love, stay happy;
I pray you heal fast.
